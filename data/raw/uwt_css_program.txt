UW Tacoma Computer Science and Systems Program

The Computer Science and Systems program prepares students for careers in software development, information technology, cybersecurity, data science, and related fields.

Students complete coursework in programming, data structures, algorithms, software engineering, operating systems, databases, networking, and computer architecture.

Many courses include group projects that simulate real-world software development environments.

Students gain experience working with programming languages, development tools, testing techniques, and collaborative workflows.

The curriculum encourages critical thinking, problem solving, and communication skills.

Graduates pursue careers in software engineering, systems administration, cloud computing, cybersecurity, and technology consulting.

Internships, personal projects, and extracurricular learning are commonly recommended to strengthen career readiness.
